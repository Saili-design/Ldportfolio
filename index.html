Process
How I build a level
02 / 04

2D Sketch First

When there's no directive, I start on paper, sketching a rough whitebox in Miro or an illustration tool before ever opening the engine, so the first pass is intentional, not improvised.

Ideation

Whitebox Validation

Every idea is tested as a whitebox before becoming anything final. Scale and flow are checked against the sketch, with room to revise before committing to a full greybox pass.

Validation

Silent Player Guidance

Lighting, landmarks, save points, combat triggers and loot drops all double as direction. The player feels free while being quietly steered exactly where the level needs them.

Guidance

Built-In Rhythm

Every level breathes at a deliberate pace, alternating tension and release, engineered to keep players in the session longer and leave them with something worth talking about.

Pacing

Engine-Agnostic

Primarily Unreal Engine, with hands-on Unity experience. Comfortable picking up an internal studio engine or any external tool outside what I already know, since editor logic and design intent carry across software.

Tools

Iterate Until It's Right

It's common for me to build 4 or 5 different layouts for the same space, playtesting each, comparing outcomes, and keeping only the version that earns its place in the final level.

Iteration

What I bring to a level
03 / 04
01

Combat & Encounter Design

Enemy placement, arena shaping, crowd control, and difficulty pacing built to stay tense without ever becoming unfair.

02

Exploration & Traversal

Multi-path layouts, verticality, and hidden routes that reward curiosity without ever losing the critical path.

03

Economy & Progression Systems

Loot drops, save points, and unlock pacing placed directly into the level layout, not bolted on as an afterthought.

04

Tension & Pacing Control

I build levels around a deliberate tension curve: raising pressure through combat or risk, then releasing it with safety or exploration. Keeping a player's heartbeat moving, not flatlining, is what turns a level into something memorable.

Indie Studio · Funding Demo

Alys Redemption

Full Level Design & Environment Art · ~1 Year Production

A 20-minute vertical-slice demo built to secure project funding, designed to showcase combat dynamics, the game's economy systems, crowd control, and enemy encounters in a single continuous experience.

A dark fantasy world blending influences from The Witcher with the poetic, forest-driven atmosphere of Ori and the Blind Forest. The level started as a 2D layout sketched in Miro, moved into whitebox validation with the team, then a full greybox pass, before every block was replaced with final 3D assets modeled in Blender alongside the art team.

I owned the full level design and environment art for this project: encounter placement, combat arenas, crowd control spacing, economy and loot integration, and the cinematic beats woven through the level sequence.

Indie Studio Project · Saudi Arabia

C_Temple · Whitebox

Full Creative Freedom · Survival & Exploration

Commissioned by an independent studio based in Saudi Arabia for a survival and exploration game set in the desert. I was given full creative freedom over the level design, starting outdoors before transitioning the player into a temple interior.

The brief was deceptively simple: make the player feel completely free to explore, while quietly guiding them toward the temple without ever feeling directed. Scale, silhouette, and landmark placement carried that weight instead of UI markers or waypoints.

Client Project · Switzerland

Protocol · Sci-Fi Blockout

2D Floor Plan to 3D Playable Level · Platformer / Action

The client, an independent Swiss developer building his second game, came with his own 2D drawings and a clear base idea for the level. My job was translating that idea into a fully realized 3D space: a traced critical path running through multiple floors, with every element placed deliberately to support a platformer/action experience.

The level spans connected floors with neon-marked zones used as wayfinding, integrating objective placement and multi-path routing directly into the layout, preserving the client's original design intent while solving real scale and traversal challenges.
